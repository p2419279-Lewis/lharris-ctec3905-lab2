    A Brief History of South Africa

    By Lewis Harris (Currently work in progress!!!)

    Aprox. 3 million - 70,000 BC - Prehistory:
    South Africa has some of the oldest human-fossils found by archeologists, indicating that it might be the birthplace of modern humans. Caves in the Gauteng Province are so rich in early human fossils that they have been given the name "The Cradle of Humankind". In 1924, a 2.51 million year old fossilised skull of an early hominin "Taung Child" was discovered in the cradle near Taung, and was dubbed "One of the most important discoveries of the 20th Century".

    Homo erectus are believed to have expanded and migrated out of Africa around 1.75 million years ago, while Homo sapiens appeared to occupy all of Africa 150,000 years ago and expanded out of Africa 125,000 - 70,000 years ago.
